Experiment 023 — Epsilon-Exploration Astrocytes

Date: 2026-02-23  |  Duration: 243.0s  |  ε = 1%

Hypothesis

Experiments 021–022 showed cluster 1 (output-side) astrocytes go dormant regardless of scoring mechanism: they lack any way to bootstrap because their activation scores are always below threshold, and they can only learn that their territory is productive by participating in learning first. Epsilon exploration breaks this deadlock: each astrocyte fires with probability ε=1% every step, independent of score and threshold. Over 50,000 steps, each dormant astrocyte receives ~500 activations. If its territory is useful, the success rate rises, the threshold drops, and regular activation takes over. If not, it stays at background exploration rate — quiet, not dead.
